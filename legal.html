📜 Understanding Licensing

🎤 For Artists

When you upload music to Swazi Music Hub, you retain ownership of your work. You grant us the right to:

  • Stream your music on our platform
  • License your music to third parties (with your approval)
  • Collect royalties on your behalf
  • Promote your music through marketing channels

🏢 For Businesses

If you want to use Swazi music in your business, you need appropriate licenses:

  • Background Music: For retail stores, restaurants, offices
  • Advertising: For commercials and promotional videos
  • Events: For corporate functions and parties
  • Digital Content: For company videos and presentations

Contact our business licensing team: business@swazimusichub.com

🎸 Cover Songs

Want to record and distribute a cover of a Swazi song?

  • You need a mechanical license
  • Royalties are paid to the original songwriters
  • We can help obtain licenses through our partners

Contact: covers@swazimusichub.com

❓ Frequently Asked Questions

Can I use Swazi Music Hub music in my YouTube video? ▼
For personal, non-monetized videos, you need a Content Creator license. For monetized channels, please contact our licensing team.
How much do artists earn from streams? ▼
Artists earn approximately E0.03 per stream on average. Revenue is split 70% to artists, 30% to platform operations.
Can I play Swazi Music Hub at my wedding? ▼
Yes! Personal events like weddings are covered under personal use. For commercial events (charging admission), you need an Event License.
What's the difference between Master and Sync licenses? ▼
A Master License gives you permission to use the specific recording. A Sync License allows you to synchronize the music with visual content. Most commercial uses require both.
